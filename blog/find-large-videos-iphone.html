How to Find Large Videos on iPhone

June 2026 • 5 min read

Your iPhone is probably storing gigabytes of video files you forgot about. A 4K video eats roughly 400 MB per minute. A few long clips from a vacation or kids' recital can consume more space than your entire photo library. Here's how to find those space hogs and clear them out.

Why Videos Take So Much Space

Videos are the single largest file type on most iPhones. Here's why:

  • 4K resolution — iPhone cameras shoot at 3840×2160. That's four times the pixels of 1080p, which means four times the data per frame.
  • High frame rates — 60 fps recording doubles the file size compared to 30 fps.
  • HDR and ProRes — Cinematic Mode, HDR video, and especially ProRes encoding produce massive files. A 30-second ProRes clip can top 6 GB.
  • Length adds up — Most people don't realize how long their videos are. A 10-minute 4K clip is roughly 4 GB. Five of those is 20 GB gone.

💡 Quick math: If your phone says "Storage Full" and you have even 10–20 videos longer than 2 minutes, those videos are almost certainly the culprit.

Method 1: Check Storage Breakdown

iOS gives you a rough overview of what's eating your storage. It won't show individual video sizes, but it confirms whether video is the problem:

  1. Open Settings
  2. Tap General → iPhone Storage
  3. Wait for the bar graph to load
  4. Tap Photos or Videos to see how much space media is using

If "Photos" (which includes videos) is the largest category, you know where to look.

Method 2: Sort Videos by Size in Photos

The Photos app doesn't have a true "sort by size" view, but you can get close:

  1. Open the Photos app
  2. Tap Albums → MEDIA TYPES → Videos
  3. Scroll through your video list — longer clips tend to appear toward the top if you recently took them
  4. Tap a video and look at the info (swipe up or tap the ℹ️ icon) to see the file size and duration

This is tedious. You have to check each video individually. For 50+ videos, it's not practical.

Method 3: Use the Files App

If you've saved videos to the Files app, you can check sizes there:

  1. Open Files
  2. Browse to On My iPhone → look for video files
  3. Tap the list view icon — it shows file sizes in columns
  4. Long-press a file → Get Info to see full details

This only covers videos you've manually saved to Files, not your camera roll.

How to Prevent Large Videos From Piling Up

  1. Record in 1080p instead of 4K — For casual videos, 1080p looks great and takes a quarter of the space: Settings → Camera → Record Video → 1080p HD at 30 fps
  2. Turn off HDR Video unless you need it: Settings → Camera → Record Video → toggle off HDR Video
  3. Delete after sharing — Once you've sent a video to someone, delete the original. You already shared it.
  4. Review monthly — Set a monthly reminder to scan for large videos before they silently eat your storage
  5. Offload to iCloud or a computer — Move long videos to your Mac or external drive and remove them from the phone

FAQ

What counts as a "large" video on iPhone?

Anything over 500 MB. A 2-minute 4K clip is roughly 800 MB. A 5-minute ProRes clip can exceed 2 GB. If you're low on space, any video over 1 minute worth of 4K is worth reviewing.

Can I compress videos on iPhone instead of deleting?

Yes, but iOS doesn't offer a built-in way to reduce video resolution after recording. You'd need a third-party app or convert on a Mac. For most people, deleting videos you no longer need is faster and more effective.

Does Dupes delete videos permanently?

You choose. Dupes can send deleted videos to the Recently Deleted folder, where they stay for 30 days. You can recover them if you change your mind. Or you can opt for permanent deletion if you're certain.

Will deleting videos affect iCloud Photos?

If you use iCloud Photos, deleting a video on your iPhone removes it from all your devices synced to that Apple ID. The video goes to Recently Deleted first, so you have a 30-day window to recover it.
