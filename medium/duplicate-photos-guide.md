# How to Find & Delete Duplicate Photos on iPhone

*Your iPhone photo library is full of duplicates. Burst shots, multiple takes, screenshots you saved twice — they add up fast. Here's how to find and remove them.*

---

## Method 1: Use iOS Built-in Duplicates Feature

Apple added a Duplicates album in iOS 16. Here's how to use it:

1. Open the **Photos** app
2. Tap **Albums** at the bottom
3. Scroll down to **Utilities** and tap **Duplicates**
4. You'll see groups of duplicate photos. Tap **Select**, then choose which ones to merge or delete

> **💡 Limitation:** Apple's built-in feature only finds *exact* duplicates — identical files. It won't catch similar photos, burst shots, or blurry versions of the same scene.

## Method 2: Find Similar Photos Manually

Beyond exact duplicates, most people have dozens of similar photos from the same event or scene. Here's how to find them:

1. Go to **Photos** → **Library**
2. Scroll through and look for sequences — burst shots, multiple angles, repeated selfies
3. Tap a photo, then swipe to see neighbors — similar photos are usually grouped together by time
4. Tap **Select** and mark the ones you don't need

This works but takes a lot of time. If you have thousands of photos, it's not practical.

## How Duplicate Detection Works

Dupes uses **perceptual hashing** — a technique that identifies images based on their visual content, not their file data. This means it can find:

- Exact copies (same file, different names)
- Near-identical photos (different sizes or slight edits)
- Photos from burst mode that look almost the same

Similar photos use **Vision framework feature prints** — Apple's built-in image analysis. Photos with similarity above 85% are grouped together.

## How Blurry Photo Detection Works

Dupes calculates a **Laplacian variance score** for each photo. Lower scores mean blurrier images. The app categorizes them as:

- **Blurry** — slightly out of focus
- **Very Blurry** — obviously unusable

You can adjust the threshold to be more or less aggressive about what counts as blurry.

## How to Prevent Duplicate Photos in the Future

1. **Turn off Keep Normal Photos** for Live Photos: Settings → Camera → Preserve Settings → toggle off Keep Normal Photos
2. **Be selective with burst mode** — don't hold the button down unless you need to
3. **Regular cleanup** — run a scan once a month before duplicates pile up
4. **Delete immediately** — if a photo is bad, delete it right away instead of "I'll clean it up later"

## FAQ

**Will deleting duplicate photos free up a lot of space?**

It depends on your library. Most people with 10,000+ photos have 5–15% duplicates. On a 256 GB phone, that's potentially 10–30 GB of wasted space.

**Does Dupes upload my photos?**

No. Everything happens on your iPhone. No cloud processing, no server uploads, no data collection.

**What if I accidentally delete a photo?**

Dupes can move deleted photos to the Recently Deleted folder, where they stay for 30 days before permanent deletion. You can also choose permanent deletion if you're sure.
