10 Ways to Free Up iPhone Storage in 2026

June 2026 • 8 min read

You're out of storage again. Your iPhone keeps pestering you about iCloud, you can't download new apps, and photos won't sync. Sound familiar? Here are 10 practical ways to reclaim storage — ranked from quick wins to deeper cleanups.

1. Offload Unused Apps

iOS can automatically remove apps you haven't opened in a while while keeping their documents and data. When you reinstall, everything comes back.

  1. Go to Settings → General → iPhone Storage
  2. Tap Enable Offload Unused Apps at the top
  3. Or scroll down, tap individual apps, and choose Offload App

This alone can free up several gigabytes without losing anything important.

2. Clear Safari Cache and Data

Safari stores website data, cache, cookies, and history — and it adds up over time. A year of casual browsing can eat 1–2 GB.

  1. Open Settings → Safari
  2. Tap Clear History and Website Data
  3. Confirm the action

💡 Note: This will sign you out of most websites. If you use iCloud Keychain, your saved passwords are safe.

3. Delete Old Messages and Attachments

Messages with photos, videos, and files are storage hogs. A conversation with years of media attachments can easily exceed 5 GB.

  1. Open Messages and find old threads
  2. Tap the contact name at the top → Info
  3. Scroll to see all attachments — review and delete what you don't need
  4. Or go to Settings → Messages → Keep Messages and set it to 1 Year or 30 Days

4. Manage Your Downloads Folder

If you download files from Safari, Mail, or other apps, they pile up in the Files app. PDFs, zip archives, images from email — they accumulate without you noticing.

  1. Open the Files app
  2. Tap Browse → On My iPhone → Downloads
  3. Sort by size and delete anything you no longer need

5. Enable iCloud Photos Optimization

iCloud Photos can store your full library in the cloud while keeping smaller versions on your phone. When you view a photo, it downloads the full version temporarily.

  1. Go to Settings → Photos
  2. Enable iCloud Photos
  3. Select Optimize iPhone Storage

This is one of the most effective ways to free up space if you have a large photo library. It won't delete anything — it just stores smarter.

6. Delete Large Attachments in Mail

The Mail app keeps attachments from emails even after you've deleted the email itself. Large PDFs, presentations, and image archives can sit in the Mail cache for months.

  1. Go to Settings → General → iPhone Storage
  2. Tap Mail and review the Documents & Data size
  3. Consider removing the account and re-adding it, or use a third-party mail app that manages storage better

7. Remove Old Podcast Episodes

If you subscribe to podcasts, downloaded episodes stick around after listening. A few podcast subscriptions can quietly eat 2–5 GB.

  1. Open the Podcasts app
  2. Tap your Library → find shows with downloaded episodes
  3. Swipe left on episodes and tap Delete Download
  4. Go to Settings → Podcasts → toggle off Download on Wi-Fi to prevent future buildup

8. Clean Up Your Photo Library

Photos are usually the biggest storage consumer. Duplicate photos, blurry shots, screenshots, and burst-mode sequences waste gigabytes — and most people never go back to clean them up.

  1. Open Photos → Albums → scroll to Duplicates under Utilities and merge them
  2. Delete blurry photos, screenshots you no longer need, and screen recordings
  3. Review Recently Deleted to actually free the space

9. Delete Apps You Don't Use

Be honest — when's the last time you opened that fitness app you downloaded in January? Or the game you played once?

  1. Go to Settings → General → iPhone Storage
  2. Sort by Last Used Date
  3. Delete apps you haven't opened in 30+ days

If you're unsure, offload instead (see tip #1). You can always reinstall later without losing data.

10. Stream Instead of Download

Downloading music, movies, and TV shows for offline viewing is convenient but expensive on storage. Streaming uses almost none.

  • Music: Use Apple Music, Spotify, or YouTube Music streaming instead of downloading full albums
  • Video: Stream Netflix, Disney+, or Apple TV+ instead of keeping downloaded episodes
  • Podcasts: Listen to episodes and delete them instead of building a library

If you do need offline access (flights, commutes), download just what you need and delete it right after.

The Quick Checklist

Here's a summary you can follow in order — from fastest wins to biggest impact:

  1. Enable offload unused apps — 30 seconds, instant space back
  2. Clear Safari cache — 30 seconds, typically 1–2 GB
  3. Enable iCloud Photo optimization — 2 minutes, huge impact
  4. Delete old messages and attachments — 5 minutes, 1–5 GB
  5. Review Downloads folder — 3 minutes
  6. Remove old podcast episodes — 2 minutes
  7. Delete unused apps — 5 minutes
  8. Clean up photos — 5–10 minutes, biggest payoff
  9. Switch to streaming — behavioral change, long-term savings

FAQ

How much space can I realistically free up?

Most people recover 5–20 GB by combining these methods. The biggest wins come from iCloud Photo optimization and cleaning the photo library, especially if you have thousands of photos.

Will offloading apps delete my data?

No. Offloading removes the app but keeps all documents and data. When you reinstall, everything is restored as if nothing happened.

Is clearing Safari cache safe?

Yes, but you'll be signed out of websites. Your saved passwords in iCloud Keychain remain intact. Bookmarks and reading list are not affected.

What if I need more than one of these fixes?

They all stack. Enable offload, clear cache, optimize photos, delete old messages, and clean your library — combined, they can transform a full phone into one with breathing room.
