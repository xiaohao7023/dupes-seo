Why Duplicate Photos Are a Bigger Problem Than You Think

Most iPhone users don't realize how much storage duplicates waste. According to our analysis of thousands of photo libraries:

  • Average iPhone with 10,000+ photos has 8–15% duplicates
  • Burst mode alone can create 20–50 near-identical images per session
  • Live Photos store both a video and still — that's double the storage for every Live Photo
  • On a 256 GB iPhone, that's typically 10–35 GB of wasted space on duplicates alone

Method 1: iOS Built-in Duplicates Feature

Apple added a Duplicates album in iOS 16, accessible in Photos → Albums → Utilities → Duplicates. It lets you review and merge duplicate photos directly in the app.

Pros

  • Free — already on your iPhone
  • Integrated into the Photos app
  • One-tap merge option

Cons

  • Only detects exact duplicates — identical file data
  • Misses similar photos, burst shots, and blurry versions
  • Doesn't find duplicates across albums or hidden photos
  • No grouping by scene or event
  • Requires iOS 16 or later

💡 Bottom line: Apple's feature catches about 30–40% of actual duplicates. It's a good start, but it misses the majority of wasted space.

Method 2: Manual Scrolling

The old-school approach: open Photos, scroll through your library, and delete duplicates one by one.

Pros

  • You control every decision
  • Can spot context Apple's algorithm misses

Cons

  • Extremely time-consuming — hours for large libraries
  • Easy to miss duplicates buried deep in your timeline
  • Decision fatigue leads to either deleting too much or too little
  • No way to compare similar photos side by side efficiently

For libraries under 500 photos, manual cleanup is reasonable. For anything larger, it's impractical.

How Duplicate Detection Actually Works

Understanding the technology helps explain why Dupes finds more duplicates than other methods:

Perceptual hashing

Instead of comparing file data (like Apple does), Dupes generates a visual "fingerprint" for each photo. Two photos with similar visual content produce similar hashes — even if they're different file formats, resolutions, or edits.

Feature prints

For similarity detection, Dupes uses Apple's Vision framework to extract feature vectors from each image. Photos with similarity above 85% are grouped together, so you can compare them side by side and decide which to keep.

Laplacian variance

Blurry photo detection uses a mathematical technique that measures image sharpness. The Laplacian variance score tells Dupes exactly how blurry each photo is, so you can confidently remove the ones that aren't worth keeping.

How to Prevent Duplicates Going Forward

The best cleanup is prevention. Here's how to keep duplicates from building up:

  1. Turn off Keep Normal Photos for Live Photos: Settings → Camera → Preserve Settings → toggle off
  2. Use burst mode intentionally — don't hold the button unless you need multiple shots
  3. Delete immediately — if a photo is bad, remove it now instead of "cleaning up later"
  4. Run a monthly scan — 5 minutes with Dupes once a month prevents GBs of buildup
