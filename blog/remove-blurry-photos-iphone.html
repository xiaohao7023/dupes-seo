How to Remove Blurry Photos from iPhone

June 2026 • 5 min read

Every iPhone photo library has them — blurry shots you meant to delete but never got around to. They waste space, clutter your Camera Roll, and bury the photos you actually want. Here's how to find and remove them for good.

Why Do Photos Come Out Blurry on iPhone?

Blurry photos happen for a few common reasons:

  • Camera shake — you moved the phone slightly when tapping the shutter. This is the #1 cause, especially in low light when the shutter stays open longer.
  • Subject motion — the person or object moved during the exposure. Kids, pets, and street scenes are especially prone to this.
  • Missed focus — the camera locked onto the wrong subject (a wall behind your friend, a reflection instead of a face).
  • Dirty lens — fingerprints and smudges on the lens create a soft, hazy look that can be mistaken for "artistic" blur.
  • Low light noise reduction — in dark environments, the iPhone applies heavy noise reduction that smears fine detail, making photos look soft.

Knowing the cause helps prevent future blur, but it doesn't fix the hundreds of blurry photos already sitting in your library.

How to Find Blurry Photos Manually

There's no "Blurry" album in the Photos app, so you have to scroll and judge by eye:

  1. Open the Photos app and go to your Library
  2. Scroll through and look for shots that are noticeably soft, motion-blurred, or out of focus
  3. Tap to zoom in if you're unsure — blur is often hard to spot at thumbnail size
  4. Tap Select, mark the blurry ones, and delete

💡 The problem: Human eyes are bad at judging blur on small screens. By the time you've scrolled through thousands of photos, you've missed half of them — or given up entirely.

How Dupes Detects Blurry Photos Automatically

Dupes uses a Laplacian variance algorithm — the same technique used in computer vision and machine learning pipelines to detect edge sharpness. Here's what it actually does:

  1. Edge detection — the algorithm applies a Laplacian filter to the image, which highlights edges and fine details. Sharp photos have strong, well-defined edges. Blurry ones don't.
  2. Variance scoring — it then measures the variance of those edge responses. High variance = lots of sharp detail = good photo. Low variance = smooth, detail-free areas = blurry photo.
  3. Categorization — Dupes classifies each photo based on its score:
    • Sharp — clear, well-focused photo
    • Slightly soft — minor blur, might still be usable
    • Blurry — noticeably out of focus or motion-blurred
    • Very blurry — clearly unusable

All of this happens on your device — no photos are uploaded or processed in the cloud.

How to Prevent Blurry Photos

  1. Clean your lens — wipe the camera lens with a soft cloth before shooting. This alone fixes a surprising number of "blurry" photos.
  2. Hold steady — tuck your elbows in, use both hands, and exhale before tapping the shutter.
  3. Use Burst Mode for action — hold the shutter button to take rapid-fire shots. You'll get at least one sharp frame.
  4. Enable Live Photos — the iPhone picks the sharpest frame from the Live Photo capture automatically.
  5. Tap to focus — don't trust the camera to pick the right subject. Tap on the person or object you want sharp.
  6. Regular cleanup — run a Dupes scan once a month so blurry photos never pile up again.

FAQ

Can Dupes tell the difference between artistic blur and accidental blur?

Not yet — Dupes scores technical sharpness, not artistic intent. If you intentionally use blur (portrait mode, motion effects), you may want to manually uncheck those in the results.

Does Dupes upload my photos to detect blur?

No. Everything happens on your iPhone. The Laplacian analysis runs entirely on-device. No cloud processing, no server uploads, no data collection.

How many blurry photos does the average person have?

Most people with 5,000+ photos have somewhere between 200 and 800 blurry or soft shots. That's real storage space and real clutter.

What if I delete a blurry photo I later want back?

Dupes can move deleted photos to the Recently Deleted folder, where they stay for 30 days before permanent deletion. You can recover them at any point during that window.
