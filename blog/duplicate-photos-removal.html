How to Find & Delete Duplicate Photos on iPhone

Updated June 2026 · 5 min read

Your iPhone storage is full again. Sound familiar? Before you start deleting precious memories, check for duplicate photos — they're one of the biggest hidden storage hogs on most iPhones.

Studies show that the average iPhone user has 10-20% duplicate or near-duplicate photos in their library. That could be 5-15 GB of wasted space.

Why Do Duplicate Photos Happen?

  • Multiple bursts: Taking several shots in a row and keeping all of them
  • iCloud sync issues: Photos getting duplicated during sync between devices
  • Photo imports: Importing the same photos multiple times from different sources
  • App behavior: Some apps save copies of photos you've already taken
  • iOS updates: Occasionally creating duplicates during system updates

Method 1: Manual Search (Free but Slow)

You can manually scroll through your photo library and look for duplicates, but this is incredibly time-consuming for libraries with thousands of photos.

Steps:

  1. Open the Photos app
  2. Go to Albums → All Photos
  3. Scroll through and look for similar images
  4. Tap Select, then tap the duplicate photos
  5. Tap the trash icon to delete

Downside: This can take hours for large libraries and you'll likely miss similar (not exact) duplicates.

Method 2: Use the Built-in "Duplicates" Album (iOS 16+)

Apple added a Duplicates album in iOS 16, but it has limitations:

  • Only finds exact duplicates, not similar photos
  • Requires iOS 16 or later
  • Doesn't always catch all duplicates

To find it: Photos app → Albums → scroll down to "Utilities" section → Duplicates

Tips to Prevent Future Duplicates

  1. Be selective with bursts: After taking burst shots, pick the best one and delete the rest
  2. Check before importing: Make sure you're not importing photos you already have
  3. Regular cleanup: Set a monthly reminder to scan for duplicates
  4. Use a duplicate remover regularly: Apps like Dupes can scan in seconds, so there's no excuse not to check periodically

Frequently Asked Questions

Will deleting duplicate photos affect my iCloud?

Yes, if you delete a duplicate photo from your iPhone, it will also be removed from iCloud. This is actually a good thing — it frees up iCloud storage too.

What's the difference between duplicates and similar photos?

Duplicates are identical files, while similar photos are taken moments apart (like burst shots). A good app like Dupes finds both.

Is it safe to delete duplicate photos?

As long as you keep at least one copy of each unique photo, deleting duplicates is completely safe. Apps like Dupes let you preview before deleting.
