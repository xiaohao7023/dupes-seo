10 Ways to Free Up iPhone Storage in 2026

Updated June 2026 · 8 min read

"Storage Almost Full" — the notification every iPhone user dreads. But before you start frantically deleting apps and photos, there are smarter ways to reclaim space without losing anything important.

This guide covers the 10 most effective methods to free up iPhone storage, ranked by how much space they typically save.

1. Delete Duplicate Photos (Saves 5-15 GB)

🏆 #1 Method: Duplicate photos are the #1 hidden storage hog. Most users have 10-20% duplicates in their library. A quick scan with Dupes can free up 5-15 GB in seconds.

Unlike manual searching, a dedicated app finds exact duplicates AND similar photos (burst shots, slightly different angles). Read our full guide on removing duplicate photos →

2. Offload Unused Apps (Saves 2-10 GB)

iOS can automatically remove apps you haven't used while keeping their data:

  1. Settings → General → iPhone Storage
  2. Enable "Offload Unused Apps"

The app icon stays on your home screen with a cloud symbol. Tap to reinstall when needed — your data is preserved.

3. Clear Safari Cache (Saves 1-3 GB)

  1. Settings → Safari → Clear History and Website Data

This clears browsing data but keeps your bookmarks and passwords.

4. Review Large Message Attachments (Saves 1-5 GB)

  1. Settings → General → iPhone Storage → Messages
  2. Review "Top Conversations" by size
  3. Delete old photos and videos from message threads

5. Optimize Photo Storage (Saves 5-20 GB)

Enable iCloud Photos with "Optimize iPhone Storage":

  1. Settings → Photos → Optimize iPhone Storage
  2. Full-resolution photos are stored in iCloud, smaller versions on your device

Requires iCloud+ subscription for significant savings.

6. Delete Old Downloads (Saves 1-5 GB)

Check the Files app for downloaded PDFs, videos, and documents you no longer need.

7. Clear App Cache (Saves 1-3 GB)

Many apps store cache data that can be cleared:

  • WhatsApp: Settings → Storage and Data → Manage Storage
  • WeChat: Settings → General → Storage → Clean
  • Other apps: Check their individual settings

8. Delete Old Podcasts and Music (Saves 1-10 GB)

Downloaded podcasts and music add up quickly. Remove ones you've already consumed.

9. Review and Delete Old Backups (Saves 2-10 GB)

  1. Settings → [Your Name] → iCloud → Manage Account Storage
  2. Review old device backups
  3. Delete backups from devices you no longer use

10. Reset and Restore (Nuclear Option)

If nothing else works, backing up and restoring your iPhone can clear accumulated system junk. Only do this as a last resort.
